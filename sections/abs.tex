\begin{abstract}
    Quantum control is an important logical primitive of quantum computing programs, and an important concept for equational reasoning in quantum graphical calculi.
    We show that controlled diagrams in the ZXW-calculus admit rich algebraic structure. The perspective of the higher-order map $\Control$ recovers the standard notion of quantum controlled gates, while respecting sequential and parallel composition and multiple-control.

    In this work, we prove that controlled square matrices form a ring and therefore satisfy powerful rewrite rules. We also show that controlled states form a ring isomorphic to multilinear polynomials. Putting these together, we have completeness for polynomials over same-size square matrices.
    These properties supply new rewrite rules that make factorisation of arbitrary qubit Hamiltonians achievable inside a single graphical calculus.
\end{abstract}
