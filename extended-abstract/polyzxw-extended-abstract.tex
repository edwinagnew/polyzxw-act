\begin{abstract}
We show that controlled diagrams in the ZXW-calculus admit rich algebraic structure. Controlled states form a commutative ring isomorphic to multilinear polynomials, while controlled square matrices form a non-commutative ring. These properties supply new rewrite rules that make factorisation of arbitrary qubit Hamiltonians possible inside a single graphical calculus. The higher-order map $\Control$ recovers familiar controlled gates and composes cleanly, enabling multi-control and black-box Hamiltonian reasoning without leaving the diagrammatic setting.
\end{abstract}

Quantum control is a universal primitive that underlies block encodings, LCUs and Hamiltonian simulation. Yet graphical calculi typically treat control as a gadget rather than a first-class algebraic object. We work in the qubit ZXW-calculus (complete for finite-dimensional linear maps) and add controlled states and controlled square matrices as generators. The goal is to characterise their algebraic laws and exploit them for equational reasoning.
These rewrite rules already support Hamiltonian-level reasoning in practice: they are used for Hamiltonian summation/exponentiation~\cite{shaikh2022sum}, and the copy rule \CMcpy\ has been used to derive general forms of two-body fermionic Hamiltonians under arbitrary linear encodings~\cite{mcdowallrose2025f2q}, directly relevant to electronic-structure mappings.
As a consequence, completeness for matrix-valued polynomials gives a purely diagrammatic route to factorising arbitrary qubit Hamiltonians (see Fig.~\ref{fig:factorization-proof}), including settings with black-box matrix terms. The same framework unifies ordinary controlled gates, multi-control constructions, and algebraic manipulation of controlled states in one calculus. Interpreting controlled states as multilinear polynomials also exposes direct links to classical algebraic tooling for optimization and structural analysis.

\subsection{Ring structure of controlled states}
With operations $\boxplus$ and $\boxtimes$, controlled $n$-partite states form a commutative ring. Operationally, additive identity/inverse, associativity/commutativity, and distributivity are derived directly by local rewrites rather than matrix detours.

\subsection{Polynomial semantics and complete normal form}
Arithmetic ZXW diagrams admit a canonical polynormal form (PNF), shown in Fig.~\ref{fig:pnf}. The key semantic consequence is
\[
\tilde S_n \simeq \mathbb{C}[x_1,\ldots,x_n]/(x_1^2,\ldots,x_n^2),
\]
so controlled states correspond exactly to multilinear polynomials. Equality checking reduces to rewriting both sides to PNF and comparing coefficients.
The novelty is that this work gives explicit controlled-diagram rewrite rules for that normalization pipeline. It builds on state-normal-form completeness from ZW/ZX~\cite{hadzihasanovic2017thesis,Hadzihasanovic2018zwzxcomplete}, and goes beyond prior Cartesian-Distributive analyses where explicit rule-level derivation was given only for Boolean circuits~\cite{wilson2021revderbool,wilson2023diffpolycirc}.

\subsection{Completeness for matrix-valued polynomials}
Replacing scalar indeterminates in PNF by controlled square matrices yields multivariate polynomials over same-size matrices. The same rewrite fragment remains complete in this lifted setting, enabling normalization and factorization with black-box matrix terms.

\subsection{Higher-order control map}
The map $\Control$ sends a matrix to its controlled form and preserves sequential and parallel composition. Iterating $\Control$ recovers standard multi-control gates (e.g., Toffoli from controlled-controlled-$X$), connecting circuit-level control to the rewrite theory above.

\subsection{Diagrammatic Verification of Ring Requirements}
\begingroup
\small
\paragraph{(1) Definition of addition.}
\(\scalebox{0.54}{\tikzfig{../tikz/con/csq_sum}}\).
This defines weighted controlled addition \(\widetilde{\sum_i c_iM_i}\) by control-branching.

\paragraph{(2) Definition of multiplication.}
\(\scalebox{0.54}{\tikzfig{../tikz/con/csq_prod}}\).
This defines controlled multiplication \(\widetilde{\prod_i M_i}\) by serial composition in the active sector.

\paragraph{(3) Definition of scalar multiplication.}
For \(c\in\mathbb{C}\), set \(c\odot\tilde M:=\widetilde{cM}\), the one-branch instance of (1).

\paragraph{(4) Commutativity of addition.}
\(\scalebox{0.39}{\tikzfig{../tikz/con/csq_add_comm_statement}}\).
This is \CMcom: swapping additive branches preserves the same controlled sum.

\paragraph{(5) Additive identity and additive inverse.}
Using (1), \(\tilde M\boxplus\widetilde{0}=\tilde M\), and with \((-\tilde M):=(-1)\odot\tilde M\), \(\tilde M\boxplus(-\tilde M)=\widetilde{0}\).

\paragraph{(6) Associativity of addition.}
Regrouping the same addition wiring from (1) gives
\((\tilde M_1\boxplus\tilde M_2)\boxplus\tilde M_3=\tilde M_1\boxplus(\tilde M_2\boxplus\tilde M_3)\).

\paragraph{(7) Multiplicative identity.}
By \CMzero\ with \(M=I\), the unit is \(\widetilde{I}\), with
\(\widetilde{I}\boxtimes\tilde M=\tilde M=\tilde M\boxtimes\widetilde{I}\).

\paragraph{(8) Associativity of multiplication.}
Regrouping the product wiring from (2) gives
\((\tilde M_1\boxtimes\tilde M_2)\boxtimes\tilde M_3=\tilde M_1\boxtimes(\tilde M_2\boxtimes\tilde M_3)\).

\paragraph{(9) Left and right distributivity.}
\(\scalebox{0.42}{\tikzfig{../tikz/con/csq_dist_statement}}\).
Control copying (\CMcpy) yields left distributivity; the mirrored rewrite yields right distributivity.

\paragraph{(10) Multiplicative inverse (when defined).}
Not a ring axiom. If \(M\) is invertible, then
\(\tilde M\boxtimes\widetilde{M^{-1}}=\widetilde{I}=\widetilde{M^{-1}}\boxtimes\tilde M\).
